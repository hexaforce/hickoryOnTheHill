Hiking Trails on Penang Hill

As the hill is located very close to populated areas, thousands of hikers of all levels of fitness make full use of the various trekking trials and bridle paths every day. There is also the 5 km tarred road popularly called the "jeep track", which is open only to the vehicles of the hill residents. The most popular pit stops along this route are 52 and 84. Here, hikers can get refreshments prepared by volunteers.

There are numerous trails from the lowlands up Penang Hill. Among the popular routes are the ones from Lower Station (Air Itam), from Moongate (near Botanical Gardens), and along the Jeep Track in Botanical Gardens. See Map below for details of the various trails.


Jeep Track to Penang Hill

The alternative route to Penang Hill is by the Jeep Track from Botanical Gardens. Marker points were placed along the Jeep Road to indicate the distance. Point 47 is a rest area. Point 84 is the first Tea Kiosk along this route. It offers black coffee, chinese tea, boiled water and biscuits to hikers for free. Donations are welcome.


Moniot Road

Moniot Road is of special historical and botanical significance. Early botanists of Penang collected many of the hill flora specimens now found in major herbariums around the world. One such botanist was Charles Curtis who established the 30ha (74 acres) Penang Botanical Gardens in 1884.
